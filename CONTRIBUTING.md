# Contributing to the Truth Repository

*This project belongs to no one, which means it belongs to everyone willing to help build it.*

---

## Where We Are

This is the founding vision stage. There is no code yet — only the blueprint. That means the most valuable contributions right now are **ideas, challenges, and architecture thinking**, not pull requests.

This is the moment when the foundations get set. What you say here shapes what gets built.

---

## How to Contribute Right Now

### Open an Issue
This is the primary way to contribute at this stage.

**Good reasons to open an issue:**
- You see a flaw in the constitutional protections
- You have a better model for how connection weights should work
- You know of existing technology we should build on (or avoid)
- You want to challenge an assumption in the architecture
- You have experience with distributed systems, knowledge graphs, or mesh networks
- You just want to say "I see this and I want to help"

**How to write a good issue:**
- Be specific about what you're responding to
- Say why it matters, not just what you think
- If you're challenging something, propose an alternative
- Disagreement is welcome — mandatory friction is a core design principle here

### Star the Repository
If this vision resonates with you, starring the repo signals to others that it's worth finding. It costs nothing and helps the right people discover it.

### Share It
Send it to one person who would genuinely connect with it. Not mass-sharing — one right person is worth more than a thousand casual views.

---

## Principles for Collaboration

**Consensus is not truth.** You will not be ignored for holding a minority view. If you have evidence, make your case.

**Mandatory friction is a feature.** Push back. Challenge the design. An idea that can't survive challenge shouldn't survive.

**The spark doesn't own the fire.** No contributor — including the founder — has final authority over the direction of this project. The constitutional protections exist precisely to prevent that.

**Be specific.** Vague enthusiasm and vague criticism are equally useless. Say what you actually think and why.

---

## What's Coming

When the project moves from vision to build, we'll add:
- Technical contribution guidelines
- Code style and architecture standards
- A formal governance process for constitutional amendments
- A process for the safety classification decisions

Until then — talk. The design isn't final. You're early enough to shape it.
