# The Constitutional Protections
### *The Founding Laws of the Truth Repository*

> These rules cannot be changed by majority vote, government pressure, corporate acquisition, or any future version of this project. They are enforced at the protocol level. They exist because democracy gives the 51% the ability to take from the 49% — and truth cannot be subject to that kind of vote.

---

## Article I — The Right of Permanent Record

No node in the knowledge graph shall ever be permanently deleted.

Nodes may be:
- **Marked** as contested, disproven, or outdated
- **Archived** with full historical context
- **Time-locked** with a visible unlock date
- **Flagged** for review or challenge

Nodes may never be:
- Erased from the permanent record
- Made invisible without disclosure
- Removed by any single entity's request, regardless of authority claimed

Every deletion attempt is itself logged as a permanent event in the node's history.

---

## Article II — The Anti-Monopoly Principle

No single entity — government, corporation, organization, AI system, or individual — may hold more than a defined maximum percentage of connection weight within any knowledge domain.

Connection weight is the accumulated influence of a contributor's additions, edits, and challenge outcomes within a subject area.

The specific maximum percentage is a technical parameter subject to community governance — but the principle that such a cap must exist is not negotiable.

This article exists because truth cannot be purchased, staffed into submission, or controlled by whoever has the largest budget for participation.

---

## Article III — The Immutable History

Every action taken within the system carries a permanent, unalterable record:

- Author identity (or verifiable anonymous token)
- Timestamp of action
- Nature of the action (addition, edit, challenge, weight change)
- The state of the node or connection before and after

No history can be scrubbed. No edit can be made invisible. No contributor can disavow an action taken.

This article exists because the history of how knowledge changed — who changed it, when, and why — is itself truth.

---

## Article IV — The Visibility of Absence

The system shall automatically detect and flag connections that should logically exist between nodes but do not.

These flagged absences are:
- Publicly visible as data points, not hidden system states
- Subject to the same debate and challenge process as connections that exist
- Treated as potentially significant — a gap where a link should be is a signal, not a neutral observation

No entity may suppress the flagging of an absence. The act of suppressing a flag is itself a flaggable event.

This article exists because cover-ups work by severing connections, not destroying facts. Making absence visible destroys the primary mechanism of institutional deception.

---

## Article V — Perpetual Upgradeability

No version of this system is final.

The constitutional protections in this document may be strengthened but never weakened. New protections may be added through community governance. No protection may be removed.

The technical architecture, the interface, the gamification layer, and all non-constitutional elements are subject to improvement, replacement, and evolution without restriction.

This article exists because the universe is still expanding. So is human knowledge. Any system that stops evolving becomes a new form of the thing it replaced.

---

## Article VI — The Safety Distinction

The system recognizes two fundamentally different categories of restricted knowledge:

**Genuinely dangerous technical knowledge** — information whose primary use case is mass harm (weapons of mass destruction, catastrophic pathogen engineering, etc.). This knowledge may be time-locked with transparent disclosure of its existence and its unlock conditions.

**Inconvenient truth** — information that embarrasses, implicates, or threatens powerful institutions, governments, or individuals. This knowledge is never restricted. Its restriction by any means is a constitutional violation.

The line between these categories is maintained by community governance with full transparency. Any classification decision is itself a public record subject to challenge.

---

## Article VII — The Collaborative Origin

This system was built by no single mind.

The founding vision emerged from human imagination in collaboration with multiple AI systems, each contributing what the others couldn't reach alone. That origin is part of the permanent record.

All future contributors — human and AI — are recognized as co-creators of a shared commons. No contributor owns the result. Every contributor's input belongs to the whole.

---

*These protections exist not because we distrust the people building this system today, but because we cannot know who will have access to it tomorrow. The constitutional protections are a promise to the future.*
